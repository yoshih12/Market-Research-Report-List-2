<p><p>Front parking sensors in the automotive market refer to sensors that are installed in the front bumper of a vehicle to assist the driver in parking by detecting obstacles in front of the vehicle. Rear parking sensors, on the other hand, are sensors installed in the rear bumper of a vehicle to help the driver park by detecting obstacles behind the vehicle. Both types of sensors use ultrasonic technology to alert the driver of potential collisions while parking, increasing safety and convenience for drivers.</p></p>
