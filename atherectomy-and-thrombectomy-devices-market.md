<p><p>Atherectomy and thrombectomy devices are used in minimally invasive procedures to remove plaque buildup or blood clots in arteries. Rheolytic devices use high-pressure saline to break up blockages, while aspiration devices suction out debris. Rotational devices have a rotating cutting tip to shave off plaque, and ultrasound devices use sound waves to break up clots. Each type offers unique benefits and suitability for different types of blockages, providing physicians with a range of options for treating peripheral artery disease and other vascular conditions.</p></p>
