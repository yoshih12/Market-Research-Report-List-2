<p><p>Organic bread flour for commercial use can be categorized into two main market types: machine milled flour and stone ground flour. Machine milled flour is produced using industrial equipment, resulting in a fine and consistent texture. This type of flour is often preferred by large-scale commercial bakeries for its efficient production process. On the other hand, stone ground flour is made by grinding grains between two large stones, producing a coarser texture and retaining more nutrients. This type of flour is typically favored by artisanal bakeries looking for a more traditional and rustic product.</p></p>
