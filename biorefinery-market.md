<p><p>Biorefinery markets can be classified into two main types: biochemical and thermochemical. Biochemical biorefineries utilize biological processes such as fermentation to convert biomass into products like biofuels and biomaterials. On the other hand, thermochemical biorefineries use heat and chemical reactions to break down biomass into fuels, chemicals, and other valuable products. Both types of biorefineries play a crucial role in the sustainable production of renewable resources and reducing reliance on fossil fuels, contributing to a more environmentally friendly economy.</p></p>
