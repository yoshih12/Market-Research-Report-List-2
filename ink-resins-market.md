<p><ul><li>Modified Rosin</li><li>Hydrocarbon</li><li>Acrylic</li><li>Polyamide</li><li>Polyurethane</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ink resins are essential components in the ink formulation process, providing adhesion, durability, and gloss to the final product. The main types of ink resins include Modified Rosin, Hydrocarbon, Acrylic, Polyamide, and Polyurethane. Modified Rosin resins are derived from natural sources and offer good adhesion properties. Hydrocarbon resins provide excellent color retention and flexibility. Acrylic resins are known for their fast drying time and water resistance. Polyamide resins offer superior abrasion resistance. Polyurethane resins provide exceptional durability and chemical resistance. Each type caters to different ink formulation needs in various industries.</p></p>
