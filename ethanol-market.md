<p><ul><li>Corn Based Ethanol</li><li>Cellulosic Ethanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ethanol market consists of two main types: corn-based ethanol and cellulosic ethanol. Corn-based ethanol is produced from the fermentation of corn starch, while cellulosic ethanol is made from the conversion of cellulose-rich feedstocks like agricultural residues and wood chips. Corn-based ethanol has been more prevalent in the market due to its easier production process and lower costs, while cellulosic ethanol is considered a more sustainable alternative due to its utilization of non-food biomass sources. Both types play a significant role in the renewable fuels industry.</p></p>
