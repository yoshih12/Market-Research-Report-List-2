<p><p>Photochromic films are categorized into three main types: Organic, Inorganic, and Organic-Inorganic Hybrid. Organic photochromic films are made from carbon-based materials like polymers, while inorganic films are composed of non-carbon compounds like metal oxides. Organic-inorganic hybrid films combine the properties of both organic and inorganic materials. Each type of film has unique characteristics and applications, with organic films being more flexible and lightweight, inorganic films offering high durability and stability, and hybrid films combining the best of both worlds.</p></p>
