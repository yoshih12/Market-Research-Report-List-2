<p><ul><li>Solution Heat Treatment</li><li>Solution Annealing</li><li>Water Quench</li><li>Polymer / Glycol Quench</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive heat treatment refers to different processes used to alter the physical and mechanical properties of automotive components. The types of heat treatment methods used in the automotive industry include solution heat treatment, which involves heating the material to a high temperature and then cooling it rapidly. Solution annealing is a similar process but involves a slower cooling rate. Water quench and polymer/glycol quench methods involve using specific quenching mediums to cool the material quickly. Other methods may also be used depending on the specific requirements of the automotive component.</p></p>
