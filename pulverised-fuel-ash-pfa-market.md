<p><p>Pulverised Fuel Ash (PFA) is a fine powder that is a byproduct of burning pulverised coal in electric power generating plants. PFA consists of particles that are small enough to be carried in the flue gases and collected in electrostatic precipitators or bag filters. <p><p>Pulverised Fuel Ash (PFA) is a byproduct of coal combustion and is categorized into two main classes: Class C and Class F. Class C PFA contains a higher percentage of calcium oxide and can be used in applications where early strength development is important, such as road construction. Class F PFA has lower calcium oxide content and is suitable for applications requiring long-term strength, such as mass concrete structures. These different classes cater to specific market needs and allow for versatile usage of PFA in various industries.</p></p>
